My journey through STEM involved many hours of hard-work which led to many achievements. These activities and awards encapsulate my profound interest in the field of computer science and have played a significant role in shaping my path in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. While it hasn’t always been easy being a female in STEM, I have always been resilient and up to the challenge of closing the gender gap in computer science.

Timeline of my STEM Journey

October 2025

Computer Science Honor Society Induction

As President, I hosted an induction ceremony for over 30 new members of the Computer Science Honor Society. I organized the event to welcome and recognize incoming students, celebrate their achievements in computer science, and foster a sense of community and engagement within the organization.

August 2025

Janelia Internship

During my internship at the Howard Hughes Medical Institute’s Janelia Research Campus, I collaborated with Dr. Aubrey Weigel and Dr. Jeff Rhoades to enhance 3D organelle segmentation in electron microscopy datasets. I created a PyTorch wrapper with a residual head to build on a pre-trained model, allowing the system to learn new organelles in a stepwise manner and improve generalization across multiple datasets. Additionally, I generated high-quality 3D segmentation masks that will be shared on the OpenOrganelle platform, contributing to open-access resources for cellular imaging research.

Presentation to MS CS Teachers

I presented STEMpowered Girls’ outreach plans to middle school computer science teachers in Loudoun County. During the presentation, I outlined our curriculum, workshops, and tutoring programs designed to expand access to coding and technology education for young girls. This opportunity allowed me to share our impact, gather feedback from educators, and build partnerships to further support students’ engagement in STEM across the district.

May 2025

ACL Hackathon

I helped organize and manage a two-day hackathon with 375 middle school and high school competitors. I coordinated a leadership team of over 20 students across subcommittees including website, workshops, sponsorship, marketing, judging, activities, attendance, graphics, and budget, ensuring the event ran smoothly and provided an engaging, educational experience for all participants.

I founded and currently lead STEMpowered Girls, a nonprofit organization dedicated to expanding access to technology education for young girls and AP Computer Science A students in Loudoun County. I have created custom curricula in Java, Python, and web development and led workshops and tutoring sessions to teach coding skills, problem-solving, and computational thinking. Through these efforts, the organization has reached and positively impacted over 150 students, fostering interest and confidence in STEM fields.

I presented my ALSIP research at the 2024 IEEE MIT Undergraduate Research Technology Conference (URTC), where my paper was published in IEEE. My participation in this conference allowed me to engage with fellow researchers, gain valuable feedback, and contribute to the advancement of knowledge in my field.

July 2024

Girl Scout Gold Award

For my Girl Scout Gold Award Project, I designed and implemented a program to empower girls in middle school to learn programming, with the goal of addressing the gender gap in STEM. I spent over 80 hours creating a custom Java curriculum that combined interactive coding lessons, hands-on exercises, and real-world problem-solving projects to engage students and build confidence in their technical skills. I personally taught 15 girls, guiding them through concepts from basic programming logic to object-oriented design, while fostering a supportive and collaborative learning environment. The project’s success inspired the creation of STEMpowered Girls, a nonprofit organization I co-founded to continue offering workshops, mentorship, and resources that encourage young girls to explore computer science and pursue STEM careers.
