My journey through STEM involved many hours of hard-work which led to many achievements. These activities and awards encapsulate my profound interest in the field of computer science and have played a significant role in shaping my path in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. While it hasn’t always been easy being a female in STEM, I have always been resilient and up to the challenge of closing the gender gap in computer science.

Timeline of my STEM Journey

October 2023

MIT Beaverworks

This fall, I was selected to be a part of an MIT Beaver Works course: CogWorks. In this program, I am learning about various machine learning concepts including advanced NumPy, image classification with Python, and neural networks. My capstone project will be a tool to sort images based on user input.

October 2023

UVA Visitation

Through the Society of Women Engineering High School Visitation program, I toured the engineering labs at UVA. The tours offered me valuable insights into doing an engineering major at UVA. This experience was inspirational as I also had the opportunity to listen to incredible speeches about imposter syndrome and the “definition” of a hacker.

July 2023

TSA Nationals

During the summer, I went to Louisville, Kentucky to compete at TSA Nationals. This was an enlightening experience as I was able to interact with interested STEM students from all around the country. In addition, my creative and unique presentation about teaching students STEM concepts won me first place in the Future Technology Teacher event.

June 2023 - July 2023

Leading Ladies Internship

Last summer, I was chosen to partake in the Leading Ladies Internship. As part of this program, I interned at Loudoun Water and I went to leadership development days where I went to STEM companies such as Sabey Data Center, Micron, and Sentara Hospital to learn about the applications of STEM in the different industries. During my internship days at Loudoun Water, I explored different technologies that played a central role in distributing water such as GIS, CCTV, and SCADA.

May 2023

American Computer Science League Finals

Winning fourth place at ACSL Nationals was a very profound experience for me as ACSL was the first programming competition that I competed in in middle school. I have come a long way from winning first place in states in middle school to winning fourth place at nationals.

May 2023

TSA States

In May, I went to Hampton, Virginia to compete in the Technology Student Association state competition. In this competition, I was a finalist for two events (Data Science and Analytics and Digital Video Production) and I won first place in one event (Future Technology Teacher). Winning first place in states gave me the opportunity to go to TSA Nationals.

March 2023

Science Olympiad States

In March, I competed in the Science Olympiad state competition at UVA. I competed in three events: Codebusters, Write It Do It, and Experimental Design. I won 2nd place in Write It Do It, 5th place in Experimental Design, and 7th place in Codebusters. Overall, my experience in Science Olympiad has been very impactful and it has deepened my love for STEM. In the upcoming school year, I will be vice-president of our Science Olympiad team, allowing me to guide other members of our team and increase their chances of winning medals.

November 2022

Math Honor Society Induction and Computer Science Honor Society Induction

In the beginning of my 9th grade school year, I was one of three freshmen in my school to be inducted to Math Honor Society. Additionally, I was also inducted into the Computer Science Honor Society at the Academies of Loudoun.

Septemper 2022

Malone Foundation Math Scholarship

At the end of my 8th grade year, I was one of 17 students in Loudoun County to be selected for the prestigious math scholarship.

April 2022

Academies of Loudoun

My acceptance into the Academies of Loudoun, specifically, the information technology path in the Academy of Engineering and Technology was a significant milestone. Attending this speciality STEM high school gives me unique opportunities to take classes such as app development, machine learning, and web development in the future.

December 2021

VEX Robotics Competition

In my 8th grade year, I joined our school’s VEX Robotics team and in December, I competed in my first VEX Robotics competition. Although we did not win any medals, our team did well considering it was our first competition.

September 2021

Volunteering at STEAM Foundation

In the beginning of 8th grade, I started volunteering at STEAM Foundation to introduce other young children to my passion for programming. While I originally started as a Java Assistant Instructor, my creative teaching methods impressed those above me and in 9th grade, I was promoted to a Java Lead Instructor. I was the youngest Lead Instructor at STEAm Foundation. My role as a Java Lead Instructor allowed me to create my own presentations and content to help kids understand Java concepts fluently.
